My Story

About Me

I am an User Experience Designer with a mathematical mind. I live in a world of introspection that requires a creative and collaborative outlet. I smile my way through life as I strive to find a fun-spirited place to call home. In my free time I play Ultimate Frisbee, board games, and watch dramedies/reality tv game shows. I read ~2 sci fi/fantasy novels a month, but am currently on a mystery novel binge. I am passionate about all things involving strategic and creative problem solving.

How I became an UX designer

Growing up, I never had any idea of what I wanted to do as a career. I always excelled at Math and Science, so I assumed I would do some type of engineering. In my first quarter at college, there wasn’t room in the calculus class I planned on taking, so I randomly took the intro computer science class instead. I found out that I had an aptitude and passion for algorithms, and threw myself at trying to get into the major. The day I got my acceptance letter was the proudest moment of my life; my hard work had paid off!

When I graduated, I was feeling lost. While I had a passion for algorithms, most of the other CSE classes hadn’t captured my imagination. I knew I needed a change. After examining my hobbies, my personality type (INFP), and what I enjoyed about coding (Algorithms, problem solving) I realized that I needed a career that involved a lot of creative problem solving. While considering creative careers, I realized the most fulfilling experiences I had from my web development job had been when I was doing user experience design without realizing it; my boss and I would often have meetings about what would be best for the user. These considerations led me to go back to school and become a UX designer!

This career change has been the best decision of my life. For the first time, I’ve found a career path that I’m really excited by. I’m treating my job search like a job. Every day I’m trying to practice skills and improve. I hope you enjoy reading over my projects! Feel free to contact me with any thoughts/considerations below.

Work Environment

I want a work environment that promotes collaboration and individual work. As an introvert, I need my time alone to think and create, but the collaborative aspects of design are often when I have the most fun. I want to work on a variety of projects that challenge me to think in different ways, and solve many unique problems. I want hard deadlines that challenge me. And most of all, I need a positive, fun, and creative team that respects my opinions and pushes me towards mastery of my trade.

K. Quinn Walker-Phelan
