Overview

Area 51 is a strugling business in the seattle furniture market. They have put all of their focus into creating their in store experience and have and outdated website. My goal was to create a modern furniture store website that would create e-commerce opportunities for Area 51.

Persona

User Journey

Ella's Journey: Ella is new to Seattle and needs furniture for her apartment she’s sharing with her boyfriend. She’s currently a student, so she is shopping on a very limited budget. She is primarily looking for a sofa currently. She pulls up Area 51’s website, and clicks on furniture. She then clicks on the sofa section. She immediately filters out all but the lowest level of price to find items that will fit her budget. Ella cares greatly about her peer’s opinions, so she next filters the results by customer ratings. She then sorts by Best Selling. After finding a few that she likes, she checks on other sites to compare prices, and then sends the one she likes to her boyfriend. She then adds it to her wishlist.

Conclusions


What I Learned

  1. This was my first UX project, and it taught me the importance of designing the entire page starting at the sketching level. While I did do many sketches of more higher arching ideas like the nav and certain pages, I didn’t think about every aspect of every page, which lead to some of the ideas being designed on the spot, rather than fully fledged out. This project has lead me to be more precise and detail oriented.
  2. This project demonstrated the importance of getting the interview script right. As an introvert, interviewing was the part of the process I had the most apprehension about, but it turns out I find it quite enjoyable. The insights that you get from people when you ask the right follow up question are very satisfying! Throughout this first interview process, I learned to cut questions that weren’t working, and to think about what questions I wasn’t getting answers to. I also added to the script to dig better into that subject.

Final Thoughts

I had so much fun doing this project. When I found designing the amount of whitespace between columns to be exciting, I knew that I was switching to the right career. This was my first experience with Sketch, and I found it to be very easy to pickup. I designed every aspect of the page for my user on this project. I wanted the navigation to be easy and familiar to someone who had shopped for furniture on other sites. I ordered filters based on how important they were to users. I tried to divide each page by percentages, and have consistent white space throughout. The importance of attention to detail in UX design was made very apparent to me while working on this.
