Overview

The San Jose Sharks app was updated last year to the chagrin of users. The new version focused more on ticket sales and sponsors rather than the team news, stats, and schedule that fans were accustomed to and wanted. As a nerdy sports fan, I decided to focus on updating the schedule, roster, statistics, standings and team page to better serve Sharks fans. I love reading analytic focused sports articles and digging into stats pages to follow the teams I support, so this project was very fun for me. Me and my team followed the 1 week design sprint for this project. (http://www.gv.com/sprint/)


Problem

The new San Jose Sharks app has a myriad of problems. It is very menu focused, but does not provide links to sections user often use: instead it focuses primarily on in stadium activities. Below, I will detail some of the problems I found with current screens of the app.

Team Information
Schedule
Roster

Team Information

While this page visually looks pretty good, having the team information page be a menu bothered users. They didn't like having to click at least twice through the app to get to any useful information.

Schedule

The schedule is presented in list form. This makes it hard to visualize when games are actually happening. A user also has to scroll to potentially see when the next game is happening. An actual calendar would give the user more information about the schedule, while also being better visually and easier to use.

Roster

Not every player is easy to find on this roster. To get to the Goalies, the user has to fully scroll down. The design also puts heavy emphasis on the number for every player, when it isn’t more important than the rest of the information.


Research


User Research

As this was a 1 week design sprint, I didn’t have time to conduct interviews. Even when there isn’t time/resources for interviews, I think it’s important to still try to get a feel for what user's think, and not just rely on gut instinct. As a replacement, I went through user reviews to collect opinions and pain points with the current app.

Competitive analysis

I examined the Blackhawks, Warriors, Trailblazers, Bucks, and NHL apps to get a sense for what other designers in the space are doing well.
