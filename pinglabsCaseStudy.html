Overview

Pinglabs is a small performance management company that focused on providing companies information about employee engagement and employee goals. We were tasked with redesigning their dashboard. Our goal was to create a professional, organized, and clean dashboard that would lead to new business for our client.


Problem

While Pinglabs customers are a fan of the questions Pinglabs sends out and the ease of responding to them by email, the current dashboard to view the data is poor. Matthew Cholerton, the owner of Pinglabs, describes the dashboard as “Unprofessional” in our first meeting with him. He also said that he was embarrassed to show potential clients the dashboard due to it’s quality. It was in dire need of a clean, professional, and organized redesign.

Research


Dashboard Design

Here are some highlights of our research:

  • Put the most important view in the top and/or upper left
  • Limit the number of views
  • One color scheme
  • Select semantically meaningful colors if they apply to the context of your data
  • Mark label options

Analysis


Affinity diagrams

From our user interviews, we created a large affinity diagram to get a sense of the trends in our user research:

Some of the biggest categories, in descending order, where:

  1. I want happy and engaged employees
  2. I want to see outliers/red flags
  3. I want a more intuitive/learnable dashboard
  4. I want my employees to be independent
  5. I want a summary along with insights on the data
  6. I want to see trends
  7. I want employees to work towards goals

Conclusions


What I Learned

  1. This project was a lesson in the importance of solving interpersonal problems quickly and in the right way. This was the first group project with team dynamic problems that i had been apart of. Two members of the team had issues with each other that cut into our productivity and lead to group wide problems. After a week of problems, I had our team have a sit down chat with the instructors to air out grievances. Had I taken actions sooner, I think we could have created an even better product.
  2. The second takeaway I had from this project was the importance of fully understanding the product one is designing. In the early design phase, some of the ideas that were presented didn’t make sense with the way Pinglabs was set up. Because some didn’t fully understand the product, we lost valuable time.

Final Thoughts

This was a very difficult and rewarding project for me. None of my team had experience working on or with a dashboard of information, and only one of us had ever used a performance management system before. We had to spend a lot of time researching dashboards and data visualization, and the product would certainly be better if we had access to a data professional. The team dynamic issues addressed above also added another layer of problems that while not fun, were interesting to learn about. All things considered, my team worked really hard and our client glowed about the final product.
